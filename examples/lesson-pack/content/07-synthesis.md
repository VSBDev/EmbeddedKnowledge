# Synthesis, alternatives, and recovery

## Synthesis

Use the Brønsted–Lowry model to track proton donation and acceptance. Use the Lewis model when electron-pair transfer is the useful description, including cases without proton transfer. A correct label is not enough: point to the changed bond, proton, or electron pair.

## Accessibility and alternatives

The diagram source and SVG are not required to understand the lesson. Their full relationships are stated in the directive long descriptions and repeated in prose. Chemical equations are also described in words. No color, animation, audio, dragging, hover, or timed interaction carries unique information.

:::{callout}
:kind: accessibility

If symbolic formulas are difficult to parse, rewrite each species as a name and mark “gains one proton” or “loses one proton.” A text-only response earns the same credit as a diagram.
:::

## Recovery route

If donor and acceptor are reversed, return to one before-and-after formula pair and circle the species that gains a proton. If the Lewis model is unclear, review lone pairs and formal charge before retrying one untimed item. Do not repeat the full set until you can explain the error.

:::{callout}
:kind: recovery

Retry `item-conjugate-pair` after explaining one prior error aloud or in writing. Unlimited attempts are allowed; use a different reaction on each retry.
:::
