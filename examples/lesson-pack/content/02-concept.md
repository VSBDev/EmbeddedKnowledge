# Proton transfer and electron-pair acceptance

## Brønsted–Lowry model

A Brønsted–Lowry acid donates a proton, and a Brønsted–Lowry base accepts a proton. When an acid loses a proton, the remaining species is its conjugate base. When a base gains a proton, the product is its conjugate acid.

:::{chemistry}
:label: chemistry-ammonia-water

\ce{NH3 + H2O <=> NH4+ + OH-}
:::

In this reaction, water donates a proton and ammonia accepts it. The conjugate pairs are water/hydroxide and ammonium/ammonia. Pair members differ by one proton.

## Lewis model

A Lewis base donates an electron pair, and a Lewis acid accepts an electron pair. Proton transfer fits this broader model because the base donates a pair toward the proton. Some Lewis reactions contain no transferable proton.
