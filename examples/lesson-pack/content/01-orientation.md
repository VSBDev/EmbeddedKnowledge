# One reaction, two models

Acid–base labels are tools for tracking a particular change. In this specimen, the first question is whether a proton moved; the second is whether an electron pair was donated and accepted.

:::{callout}
:kind: boundary

This lesson teaches chemical models. It does not support personal interpretation of blood tests, symptoms, medicines, or treatment.
:::

## Before you begin

You should be able to draw a Lewis structure and identify a lone pair. If that feels fragile, follow the recovery route in the final scene before attempting the assessment.

## Objectives

After the lesson, you should be able to identify conjugate pairs and choose the narrower model that answers a stated question.
