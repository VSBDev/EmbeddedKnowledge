# A buffer under perturbation

Consider a closed teaching model containing a weak acid `HA` and its conjugate base `A-`. A small amount of strong acid adds protons to the model.

## Make a prediction

:::{callout}
:kind: transfer
:id: transfer-buffer-response

Predict which buffer component reacts with most of the added proton. Explain the response using both conjugate-pair language and a particle-level account. State one limit of the model.
:::

## Model response

:::{equation}
:label: equation-buffer-response

A^- + H^+ \rightarrow HA
:::

The conjugate base consumes added proton to form the weak acid. This explains resistance to a sudden pH change; it does not mean pH is fixed or that buffer capacity is unlimited.

## Evidence boundary

:::{source-note}
:claims: claim-buffer-response
:sources: source-openstax-chemistry

The statement is scoped to a simplified chemical system, not a patient or biological diagnosis.
:::
