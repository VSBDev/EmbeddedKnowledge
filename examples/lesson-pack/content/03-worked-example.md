# Annotating conjugate pairs

## Question

For the reaction below, label the acid, base, conjugate acid, and conjugate base.

:::{chemistry}
:label: chemistry-acetate-water

\ce{CH3COO- + H2O <=> CH3COOH + OH-}
:::

:::{worked-example}
:id: worked-example-conjugate-pairs

1. Compare reactants and products to locate the transferred proton.
2. Acetate gains one proton, so it is the base; acetic acid is its conjugate acid.
3. Water loses one proton, so it is the acid; hydroxide is its conjugate base.
4. Check each pair: the formulas differ by exactly one proton and the charges differ by one unit.
:::

## Why the charge changes

Adding a proton adds positive charge; removing one removes positive charge. This bookkeeping check catches many reversed pair labels.
