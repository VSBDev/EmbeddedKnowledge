02

Curriculum inventory

Every discipline is visible.

Hours are nominal teaching allocations. Inquiry, practicals, retrieval, and integration overlap across modules; the completion total remains capped at 1,440 common hours.

QRS

Quantitative & scientific reasoning

180h

Algebra, units, probability, statistics, study design, uncertainty, evidence.

CHE

General chemistry

220h

Matter, bonding, stoichiometry, solutions, thermodynamics, kinetics, equilibrium, acids and redox.

BIO

Biology & genetics

250h

Cells, energy, heredity, gene expression, microorganisms, viruses and evolution.

PHY

Physics

210h

Mechanics, fluids, waves, optics, electricity, thermal and radiation physics.

ORG

Organic chemistry

150h

Structure, stereochemistry, mechanisms, biological reactions, separation and spectroscopy.

BCH

Biochemistry

200h

Proteins, enzymes, biomolecules, membranes, metabolism and biochemical regulation.

MED

Medicine-facing bridge

170h

Human structure, homeostasis, organ systems, immunity, infection and biotechnology.

INT

Integration & capstone

60h

Cross-domain cases, reproducible inquiry, public explanation and oral defence.

PW

One selected pathway

120–240h

US/MCAT, UK, Spain/continental, broad biology, advanced quantitative, or custom institution route.

03

Completion

Mastery is demonstrated, not timed.

  1. 01
    Master every required outcome

    No module average can conceal a prerequisite or safety-critical gap.

  2. 02
    Complete twelve investigations

    Physical, simulated, and dataset modalities are recorded truthfully.

  3. 03
    Complete one real pathway

    Route differences remain visible rather than being averaged into a fictional universal standard.

  4. 04
    Defend an integrative capstone

    Produce reproducible evidence, explain it to two audiences, and correct it after challenge.

BOUNDARY

Completion records learning demonstrated against this curriculum version. It is not university credit, a medical-school admission guarantee, clinical competence, or authorization to practise.
