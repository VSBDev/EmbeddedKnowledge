01

Curriculum architecture

Four stages. Ten strands.
One accountable boundary.

The hours describe academic learning and simulation. They exclude patient care, clinical service, on-call work, protected supervision, and any regulator's decision about competence.

I

Person, mind, brain & method

320h

History, lived experience, neuroscience, development, interviewing, evidence and measurement.

II

Formulation, safety & presentations

480h

Classification, medical alternatives, crisis care and the major psychiatric presentations.

III

Treatment, lifespan & continuity

400h

Medication, psychotherapy, recovery, interventions, age-specific and cross-setting care.

IV

Rights, systems & scholarship

240h

Law, culture, quality, implementation, AI, leadership, teaching and capstone integration.

PW

One area of depth

120–240h

Research, psychotherapy, lifespan, addiction, public, forensic, digital or education routes.

02

Design commitments

Classification is a tool.
The person is the subject.

  1. 01
    Lived experience changes governance

    Paid contributors help define outcomes, cases, teaching, language and assessment.

  2. 02
    Medicine and meaning stay connected

    Biology, psychology, relationships, culture and systems remain visible in every formulation.

  3. 03
    Safety includes rights

    Immediate protection, supported decisions, least-restrictive care and learning after harm are taught together.

  4. 04
    Uncertainty is assessed

    Learners compare alternatives, calibrate confidence, update decisions and state what remains unknown.

ACADEMIC-CURRICULUM BOUNDARY

The public graph and focused lesson intake are open. Curriculum review remains welcome, and every lesson still needs evidence, rights, independent review, and adjudication. Completion cannot confer residency credit, clinical competence, licensure, prescribing authority, or the title psychiatrist.
