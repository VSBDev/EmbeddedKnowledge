# Demonstrate plan design and adaptation

Complete the four items in `assessment.json` without using the concept scene. You may write, type, dictate to speech-to-text, or use another text route. No timer is required, and presentation style is not scored.

The assessment collects twenty points:

1. distinguish retrieval evidence from exposure activity: 2 points;
2. design a twelve-day plan from a fragile baseline: 6 points;
3. interpret and adapt a three-attempt record: 6 points; and
4. transfer the loop to a peer study group: 6 points.

For this lesson's local mastery gate, a provisional success is at least 16 of 20 points, including at least 4 of 6 on both plan design and transfer. This threshold is an author-created assessment convention, not a universal psychometric definition of mastery. The criterion exists to prevent a correct recognition item from compensating for an inability to construct or adapt a plan.

## Answer and feedback conditions

Attempt all items before consulting answer logic. After submission, compare each response with the analytic criteria, not only the total. For every missed criterion, write:

- what the response did;
- which planning relation is absent or incorrect; and
- the next retrieval or recovery action.

## Retry strategy

Retakes remain available. A retry must change the target topic, baseline error pattern, time horizon, or collaboration constraint so that the learner reasons again instead of replaying a memorized answer. Preserve the same outcome and scoring criteria.

## Remediation routes

- If item 1 is missed, return to the fluency-equals-mastery repair and distinguish visible production, recognition, and unaided generation.
- If item 2 scores below 4, return to the planning table and ensure every date names an operation, feedback, recovery, variation, and decision rule.
- If item 3 scores below 4, annotate each observation with “supports” and “does not support,” then redesign only the next opportunity.
- If item 4 scores below 4, return to the transfer scene and protect individual retrieval before group feedback.

After remediation, use the equivalent retry form. Do not infer durable mastery from the immediate correction alone; place a later changed task into the next course lesson or study sequence.
