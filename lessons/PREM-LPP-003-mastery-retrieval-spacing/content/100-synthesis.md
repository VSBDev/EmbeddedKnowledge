# Carry the loop into later lessons

You can now compress the design into one auditable statement:

> Define the future performance, retrieve before review, compare against criteria, recover the first material error, retrieve again after delay and meaningful variation, and let observed performance change the next opportunity.

The statement is useful only if each verb names an action in the plan. “Review on Thursday” is incomplete. “On Thursday, close the source, select a model for a changed case, justify the selection against three criteria, then repair and reschedule from the observed error” is inspectable.

## A final design audit

Before using a plan, ask:

1. **Alignment:** Does the retrieval operation match what must be done later?
2. **Evidence:** Does the attempt occur before answer review, and are criteria explicit?
3. **Recovery:** Does feedback locate a relation and prescribe a next action?
4. **Spacing:** Are opportunities distributed across the relevant horizon without claiming a universal gap?
5. **Variation:** Does a later task change a case, representation, support, or selection demand that matters?
6. **Adaptation:** Do success and error lead to different next steps?
7. **Boundary:** Is interleaving reserved for discrimination among usable categories or strategies?
8. **Inference:** Does the mastery judgment remain bounded by the task, delay, and conditions actually observed?

## Delayed and cumulative retrieval links

Use this loop again rather than ending it here:

- Within the next later lesson, retrieve the six-part loop before opening this scene.
- Apply it to one domain-specific target chosen from your diagnostic baseline.
- After a longer delay, compare the original plan with the attempt record and revise the decision rule.
- Once several models or procedures have been introduced, add mixed selection only when choosing among them is part of the target performance.

The prerequisite also remains active: a plan starts from the `topic-orientation-diagnostic-baseline` observation rather than from a confidence label alone.

## Accessibility and alternatives

Every required relationship appears in ordinary text and source order. The tables in earlier scenes repeat relationships that are also expressed as ordered lists and paragraphs. No distinction depends on color, location, motion, sound, hover, dragging, or time pressure. Learners may answer by keyboard, handwriting, speech-to-text, or another text route without changing the planning operation or rubric. At 200% zoom, narrow-screen reflow, reduced-motion settings, and print, follow the headings and lists in order; no hidden interaction contains essential content.

## Educational boundary

The loop helps design and interpret academic practice. A successful lesson assessment does not certify permanent retention, university credit, admissions readiness, or professional or clinical competence. Only later performance under the relevant conditions can add evidence for the corresponding bounded claim.
