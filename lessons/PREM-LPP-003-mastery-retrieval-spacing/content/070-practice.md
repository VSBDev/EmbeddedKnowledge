# Fade support and adapt the plan

Complete each task before reading its feedback. Support decreases across the sequence. Use your own paper, plain text, speech-to-text, or another text-producing route; the reasoning standard is the same.

## Completion task

A plan currently reads:

- Target: explain three causal links without notes in eight days.
- Baseline: fragile; link two is omitted.
- Day 0: reconstruct the explanation, compare with three link criteria, study a worked account of link two, and reconstruct again.
- Day 3: reconstruct the same wording.
- Decision rule: if all links appear, mark mastered.

Replace the day-3 line and decision rule so the plan tests more than immediate repair or repeated wording.

### Feedback

A strong completion changes the representation or case on day 3 and keeps mastery provisional. For example: “Day 3: explain the three links from a new starting condition. If link two fails, compare contrasting cases and retry sooner; if all links survive the changed case, remove prompts and schedule another cumulative attempt nearer day 8.” If you changed only the date, add a changed operation and a response to error.

## Supported task

Sam is unfamiliar with two calculation procedures and cannot yet complete either worked model. The eventual assessment will mix the procedures. Choose the better immediate action and justify it:

1. mix unsignalled problems from both procedures now; or
2. study and complete a guided model for each procedure before later mixed selection.

Use this sentence stem if needed: “The immediate operation should be ___ because ___. Interleaving becomes useful when ___.”

### Feedback

Option 2 better fits the stated baseline. Sam first needs a usable representation of each procedure. Later mixed problems can require selection once both procedures have initial support. If you selected option 1, check whether you treated interleaving as universally beneficial rather than matching it to the discrimination operation.

## Independent task

Design a seven-day plan for this fictional case:

> Noor must interpret a small graph and justify which of two already taught explanations fits. The baseline is fragile: Noor reads values accurately but selects an explanation from one memorable word rather than the relation shown by the graph.

Your plan must include:

- the target performance and criteria;
- at least three retrieval opportunities;
- feedback and recovery for the word-cue error;
- one meaningful change in graph or context;
- a stated retention horizon; and
- a decision rule after success and after error.

### Feedback checklist

Use these questions rather than comparing exact dates:

1. Does every opportunity require Noor to interpret and justify before seeing feedback?
2. Does recovery contrast the misleading word cue with the graph relation that should govern selection?
3. Does a later opportunity change the surface cue while preserving the selection demand?
4. Does error trigger targeted support and renewed retrieval?
5. Does successful delayed performance reduce support or extend to a cumulative check without becoming a claim of permanent mastery?

If any answer is **no**, revise the corresponding plan line. If all are **yes**, compare your schedule with the seven-day horizon and ensure the final attempt occurs under conditions relevant to the intended performance.

### Exemplar after your attempt

One defensible fragment is: “Day 0: Noor closes the source, interprets the graph relation, selects an explanation, and justifies the selection before checking the criteria. If the memorable word still controls the choice, compare two graphs that share that word but show different relations, then retrieve the governing relation again. Day 2: select between the same explanations for an unseen graph with changed labels. Another word-cue error triggers one supported contrasting case and an earlier renewed attempt; accurate relation-based selection removes that support and leads to a changed-context check nearer day 7.” The dates are replaceable. The important feature is that the observed word-cue error changes the recovery, the next retrieval task, and the amount of support.

## Mixed selection

For each action, label its primary job **retrieval**, **feedback**, **recovery**, or **spacing**. One action can contribute to more than one job, but choose the job that is decisive in the sentence.

1. Close the source and explain the boundary condition.
2. Compare the response against a rubric and identify the first missing relation.
3. Rebuild that relation with one contrasting worked case.
4. Schedule a changed-case attempt after a gap.

The intended primary labels are retrieval, feedback, recovery, and spacing, in that order. If two labels seemed interchangeable, state what observation would separate them. For example, a date supplies spacing only when a retrieval operation is actually scheduled on that date.
