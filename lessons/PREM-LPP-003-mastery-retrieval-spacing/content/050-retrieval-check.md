# Reconstruct the design loop

Keep the concept scene closed. Write brief answers before moving to the feedback section.

:::{check}
:id: check-reconstruct-loop
:label: Low-stakes retrieval before feedback

1. Name four components that turn a calendar entry into an interpretable retrieval opportunity.
2. Explain why a correct answer immediately after rereading does not by itself support a durable-mastery judgment.
3. A delayed attempt contains the correct conclusion but omits the governing relation. What should the feedback and next step do?
:::

## Feedback

1. A complete answer can name the target operation, attempt conditions, criteria, feedback and recovery action, spacing and variation, or a performance-linked decision rule. Any four are acceptable if they form a usable chain. If you named only dates, return to the planning table and add what the learner will generate and how the result changes the next step.
2. The attempt is too close to exposure and does not test whether the operation can be reconstructed after delay or variation. If you wrote only “memory gets worse,” make the reasoning more precise: the design needs evidence under conditions that resemble the future demand.
3. Feedback should identify the missing relation, explain why it governs the conclusion, and prescribe a targeted recovery followed by renewed retrieval. If your next step is “read everything again,” narrow it to the failed relation and then test whether the repair can be generated.

Now retrieve the six-part loop in your own order. A defensible sequence is: specify performance, retrieve, compare, recover, retrieve after delay and variation, then apply a decision rule. Different wording is acceptable when every relation remains explicit.
