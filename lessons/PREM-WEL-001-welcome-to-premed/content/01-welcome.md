# Welcome to Premed

Welcome to Premed. You are about to begin a journey through the science that makes the human body more understandable and scientific evidence more trustworthy.

You do not need to arrive knowing the subjects below. This welcome shows you what you will learn, why each subject earns its place, and what you will be able to do when the pieces come together. The course begins with careful questions, moves through matter and living systems, and ends with you using evidence to explain unfamiliar problems.

Here is the journey ahead.

- **Scientific method and laboratory practice:** You will turn curiosity into testable questions, choose variables and fair comparisons, measure carefully, keep reproducible records, and decide what a result supports. These habits matter throughout medical science because evidence must be open to inspection and challenge.
- **Mathematics and statistics:** Units, ratios, graphs, probability, averages, variation, and ranges that express uncertainty will help you read research, compare groups without hiding variation, check plausible answers, and distinguish a numerical pattern from a justified conclusion.
- **Physics:** Forces and motion; pressure, fluids, and transport; electricity; waves, sound, and light; heat; and radiation help explain movement, blood flow, electrical signals, hearing, vision, measurement, and imaging.
- **General chemistry:** Atoms, bonds, molecular shape, reactions, amounts, gases, solutions, energy changes, reaction rates, equilibrium, acids, and bases describe the molecular setting of cells and supply language and calculations for laboratory work, biology, and biochemistry.
- **Organic chemistry:** You will see how molecular shape and recurring groups of atoms influence carbon-based molecules, how electrons move in reactions, why three-dimensional arrangement matters, and how separation and instrument patterns reveal structure. This prepares you to reason about why molecules of life bind, change, and behave differently.
- **Biochemistry:** Proteins and enzymes, membranes, carbohydrates, fats, nucleotides, metabolism—the linked reactions that manage energy and materials—and biological information come together here. You will use them to explain how cells obtain and spend energy, regulate reactions, communicate, and respond to molecular change.
- **Biology and genetics:** Cell organization, membranes, division, signalling, inheritance, gene expression, variation, and evolution take you from molecules to living systems: how cells preserve information, develop different structures and functions, and generate variation across individuals and populations.
- **Human structure and physiology:** You will connect tissues and organs with their functions, then examine transport, control of internal conditions, nerves, hormones, movement, circulation, breathing, fluid balance, digestion, reproduction, and development. The aim is to explain whole-body mechanisms, not make clinical decisions.
- **Microbiology and immunology:** By comparing microbes and viruses, growth, transmission, evolution, barriers, rapid general defences, and slower targeted responses, you will explain infection and immunity as interactions among an agent, a host, an environment, and time.
- **Behaviour, society, and population health:** Perception, learning, stress, social relationships, institutions, inequality, access, environment, and population evidence widen the account of health. Biology cannot explain every pattern, and a group association does not automatically explain one person's experience.
- **Integration and capstone work:** Finally, you will bring subjects together around an unfamiliar, non-diagnostic problem; inspect sources and data; calculate or model; explain mechanisms; communicate uncertainty; and revise after critique. The goal is a defensible scientific explanation, not a pile of separate facts.

The destination is demanding but concrete. By completion, you should be able to **explain** important scientific relationships, **model** them with words, quantities, diagrams, or data, **investigate** questions with honest methods and records, and **integrate** several subjects when one alone is not enough.

For today, notice the promise inside that destination: nobody expects you to begin at the end. Curiosity, careful practice, and a willingness to correct your reasoning are enough to start.
