# Audit a changing mixture record

:::{check}
:id: check-transfer-mixture-record

A fictional materials record describes 18 blue beads and 42 white beads. After 12 blue beads are added, no beads are removed.

Before reading the feedback, determine:

1. the original blue-to-white ratio;
2. the original blue percentage of all beads;
3. the new blue percentage;
4. the percentage-point change and relative percent change in the blue share; and
5. one reverse or denominator check.
:::

## Feedback after a complete attempt

Original ratio: $18:42=3:7$. Original total: 60, so blue share is $18/60=30\%$.

New blue count: 30; new total: 72. New blue share is $30/72=5/12\approx41.67\%$.

Percentage-point increase: $41.67-30=11.67$ points. Relative increase in the blue share:

$$
\frac{41.67\%-30\%}{30\%}\times100\%\approx38.89\%.
$$

Check: a 38.89% increase applied to 30% gives about 41.67%. If you kept 60 as the new denominator, you failed to update the whole when objects were added.
