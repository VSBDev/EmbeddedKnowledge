# Two true percentages that answer different questions

A fictional archive marks 30 of 120 files for rechecking. Later it marks 36 of 120.

- The marked share rose from $30/120=25\%$ to $36/120=30\%$.
- That is an increase of **5 percentage points**.
- Relative to the old marked share, the increase is $(30\%-25\%)/25\%=20\%$.

Both 5 and 20 are correct, but they answer different questions. The denominator decides the meaning.

This lesson uses a reference-first method: label the compared quantities, name what represents one whole or the starting value, calculate, then reverse or bound the result.

## Starting check

1. Does the ratio `3 red : 5 blue` mean the same as `5 red : 3 blue`?
2. In “18 is 30% of what number?”, which value represents 100%?
3. Can a quantity increase by more than 100%?

If fractions are uncertain, read $a/b$ aloud as “a for every b” and attach the labels. If decimal arithmetic is the barrier, use exact fractions until the final conversion.

:claims: claim-ratio-quotient, claim-fraction-dimensionless

## Accessibility and alternatives

All comparisons appear in words and equations. No answer depends on color, a chart, dragging, or timing. In a nonvisual reading, say “marked files divided by all files” and name each numerator and denominator before the value.
