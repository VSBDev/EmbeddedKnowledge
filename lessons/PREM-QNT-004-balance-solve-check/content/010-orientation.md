# A plausible number that violates its equation

A fictional sampler stores 17 baseline records plus 6 records for each interval. Its total is 83 records. A dashboard reports **12 intervals**.

The verbal model is

$$
17+6n=83.
$$

Twelve sounds plausible, but substitution rejects it: $17+6(12)=89$, not 83. Solving gives $6n=66$ and $n=11$, which checks exactly.

This lesson treats an equation as a **constraint**. Solving is the controlled process of rewriting that constraint without changing which values satisfy it. The loop is:

1. represent the relationship and domain;
2. preserve equality with reversible operations;
3. isolate the requested variable;
4. substitute into the original equation and interpret.

## Starting check

- Is $3x+7$ an expression or an equation?
- Does adding 4 to only one side preserve equality?
- If a variable counts complete intervals, is $n=-2.5$ admissible even if algebra produces it?

If signed arithmetic is uncertain, write each equality-preserving step on a separate line and estimate the sign before calculating.

## Accessibility and alternatives

Every symbolic step is described in words and presented in source order. No task depends on color, spatial “movement,” dragging, or timing. Read $3(x-2)$ aloud as “three times the quantity x minus two.”
