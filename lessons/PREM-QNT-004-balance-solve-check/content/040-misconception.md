# Moving a term is shorthand, not magic

The phrase “move $-9$ across and change its sign” can hide the operation that preserves equality.

:::{misconception}
:id: misconception-moving-term

In $4x-9=15$, the $-9$ does not travel. We add 9 to **both** sides:

$$
4x-9+9=15+9.
$$

The apparent sign change is the simplified result of inverse operations. Writing the operation prevents three common failures: changing only one side, changing a sign without moving the whole term, and applying an operation to only part of a grouped expression.
:::

The same care matters in formula rearrangement. From $y=ax+b$, subtract $b$ from both sides, then divide by $a$ only if $a\ne0$:

$$x=\frac{y-b}{a},\qquad a\ne0.$$

:::{source-note}
:claims: claim-unit-independent-rearrangement
:sources: source-nist-si-chapter7

The misconception and repair are original. The resulting symbolic rearrangement preserves the source-supported quantity-equation relation without binding it to chosen units.
:::
