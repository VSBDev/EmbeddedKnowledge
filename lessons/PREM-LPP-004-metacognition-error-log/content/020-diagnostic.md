## Criterion and route

A usable reconstruction names a target performance, an unaided retrieval attempt, comparison with an answer model or rubric, a repair selected from the error, and another retrieval after a delay or bounded variation. Confidence can be recorded beside the attempt, but it becomes informative only when compared with the observed result.

If your sequence stops at rereading, use this self-contained recovery before continuing:

1. **Target:** write one exact performance that a new task will require.
2. **Retrieve:** attempt one small case without notes and commit the response before feedback.
3. **Compare:** use the supplied answer model or rubric to mark the earliest unsupported step.
4. **Repair:** practise only that step with enough support to make the decision visible.
5. **Retrieve later:** schedule a different case after a delay or bounded change in wording, values, or representation, then compare again.

If you included an attempt and feedback but not a later probe, add step 5 now. If you cannot yet produce the target, unaided attempt, comparison, repair, and later probe even with this scaffold, pause this lesson and practise the five-step loop on two fictional tasks. If the loop is available but confidence feels separate, continue: this lesson will connect the two records explicitly.
