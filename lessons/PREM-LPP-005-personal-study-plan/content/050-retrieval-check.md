## Feedback after attempting

1. **Bound capacity** prevents an unlimited task list from masquerading as a feasible week.
2. **Place dependencies and fixed work** prevents dependent problems or labs from being scheduled before their prerequisites and required preparation.
3. **Distribute learning functions** prevents one vague activity from being counted as concept learning, independent problem solving, lab evidence, and delayed review at once.
4. **Reserve and revise** prevents every disruption from becoming automatic carryover into future capacity.

The Sunday label is incomplete because it does not say what will be retrieved, whether an attempt occurs before feedback, or whether the contact is separated from the original learning. A defensible entry names the earlier target, the operation, the evidence, and the relation to the earlier episode.

Try a changed case: a plan has a Tuesday concept explanation and a Friday “review” entry that asks the learner to reconstruct and apply the concept before checking. Which pass makes the Friday contact visible, and which pass verifies that Tuesday occurs first?

Answer: distributing learning functions identifies the delayed retrieval job; dependency-aware placement checks the order. The capacity and revision passes still matter because both entries must fit and remain adaptable.

Schedule another retrieval of the four passes at the next weekly review before opening your template. Change the prompt by using a disrupted week rather than repeating these exact words.
