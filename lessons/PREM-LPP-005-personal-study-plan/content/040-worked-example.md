# Work a constrained week

This fictional example models every decision. The numbers make the reasoning inspectable; they are not recommendations for block length or weekly hours.

:::{worked-example}
:id: worked-example-twelve-block-week

**Task.** Build a week for Rowan with concept learning, problems, a supervised lab, review, and recovery. Explain the order and the next revision rule.

**Givens.** Rowan has twelve self-defined usable blocks. A supervised lab occupies two fixed consecutive blocks on Wednesday. A preparation task must precede it, and a debrief must follow it. The error log shows a `REP` representation-translation gap in a prerequisite measurement topic. A cumulative retrieval contact with an earlier node is due this week. No personal reason for the capacity limit is needed.

**Constraints and unknowns.** The fixed lab cannot move. The plan must not exceed twelve blocks. Unknowns are which flexible tasks to choose, where to place them, how much reserve to protect, and what evidence will drive revision.

**Model selection.** The four-pass model fits because the task combines a capacity limit, dependency order, distinct learning functions, and uncertainty about disruption.

**Plan.** First bound twelve blocks. Next anchor the lab and its preparation/debrief. Then protect the error-linked prerequisite repair and the due retrieval. Place concept work before dependent problems. Finally, leave two blocks as recovery capacity.

**Execution.**

| Sequence | Activity | Function and reason | Evidence or contingency |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Contrast two measurement representations | Concept and error repair; targets the `REP` prerequisite first | Explain how the same quantity appears in words and a graph |
| 2 | Complete one near problem | Problem solving after the repair | Independent representation, execution, and check |
| 3 | Retrieve an earlier node before feedback | Review; cumulative contact is due | Written reconstruction, correction after attempt |
| 4 | Prepare the supervised lab | Lab preparation before the fixed session | State variables, measurement plan, and institutional safety route |
| 5–6 | Attend the fixed supervised lab | Investigation under supervision | Record the modality actually completed; follow institutional procedure |
| 7 | Debrief the lab evidence | Lab debrief after the session | Distinguish observation, interpretation, and uncertainty |
| 8 | Solve a varied problem using the measurement relation | Independent problem solving | Select the representation and justify it |
| 9 | Retrieve the repaired representation later | Delayed review after sequence 1 | Reconstruct the relation before checking |
| 10 | Complete a mixed selection problem | Problem selection and transfer toward later work | Explain why this representation fits |
| 11–12 | Leave uncommitted | Recovery capacity | If disruption occurs, apply the reduced route or one justified revision decision |

**Checks.** Ten scheduled study blocks plus two reserve blocks equal the twelve-block boundary. Concept repair precedes both dependent problems. Preparation precedes the lab, and debrief follows it. The plan contains all five required functions. Retrieval appears before feedback and later than the relevant learning episode. No unsupervised practical route is introduced.

**Interpretation.** The plan is balanced because the functions and dependencies are visible, not because every category has equal time. It is sustainable in the bounded sense used here because it fits the declared capacity and can absorb a disruption without automatically borrowing from the next week.

**Revision rule.** At weekly review, Rowan protects incomplete prerequisite repair, fixed work, and due retrieval; moves or splits required flexible work when feasible; replaces a failed medium with an equivalent route; and removes redundant optional work. The evidence, not guilt about an unchecked box, controls the choice.

**Self-explanation prompt.** Which decision most strongly prevents sequence 8 from becoming blind trial and error, and what evidence would make you change that decision next week?
:::

The decisive choice is to place the representation repair before the varied problem. If fresh unaided evidence shows that representation is now secure, later plans can reduce that scaffold and allocate capacity to a new error or transfer task.
