# Adapt the model to an access-constrained week

The surface problem changes. You must now preserve the planning relations when the preferred medium is unreliable.

## Transfer task

A fictional learner has ten usable blocks, intermittent connectivity, and one fixed institutionally supervised practical. A cumulative review is due. The latest error-log entry shows accurate verbal recall but weak transfer to a graph. Midweek access may remove one online block.

Without using private personal details, create a text plan that:

1. bounds capacity at ten blocks;
2. places practical preparation before the fixed supervised session and debrief after it;
3. targets the graph-transfer error before a dependent problem;
4. includes concept learning, problems, lab preparation/session/debrief, delayed review, and recovery;
5. provides an offline route for every learning operation that does not require institutional equipment; and
6. states what will be protected, replaced, split, moved, or removed if the online block disappears.

Do not solve an experimental procedure or replace the supervised practical with home activity. The access adaptation concerns preparation, explanation, retrieval, graph interpretation, and response recording.

## What must remain invariant

The calendar medium can change, but these relations cannot:

- scheduled work fits the capacity boundary;
- prerequisite repair precedes dependent use;
- the fixed practical remains supervised;
- retrieval occurs before feedback and after earlier learning;
- each required learning function has evidence;
- recovery capacity or a reduced route remains explicit; and
- next-week changes follow completion and error evidence.

An equivalent offline route might download or print the bounded explanation, graph prompt, and response template during reliable access, then accept a typed or handwritten interpretation. “Look at the graph later” is not equivalent because it removes the current reasoning operation.

## Evaluate your adaptation

Read your plan chronologically. For each entry, say aloud or write: “This occurs now because …; it will produce …; if unavailable, I will ….” If the sentence has no dependency, evidence, or contingency, revise the entry rather than adding decoration.

After the week, use the fresh graph response to decide whether to fade the contrasting examples, protect another repair, or schedule a changed delayed prompt. Do not infer mastery from completion alone.
