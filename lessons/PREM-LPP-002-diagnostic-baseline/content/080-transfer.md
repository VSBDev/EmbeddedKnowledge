# Repair a familiarity-only survey

You inherit a placement form for the node “interpret measurement units.” It asks only:

> How familiar are you with measurement units? Choose 1 to 5.

The form produces a confidence-like self-rating, but it does not reveal whether a learner can retrieve, explain, or adapt the relevant knowledge. Your task is to replace it with a short, low-stakes baseline that produces interpretable node-level evidence.

## Design constraints

Your redesign must:

1. elicit an unaided response before feedback;
2. include an explanation decision and a bounded change of representation or context;
3. record confidence without letting it determine the state;
4. state unfamiliar, fragile, and secure decision rules plus one unresolved boundary;
5. route each state to a proportionate next action;
6. remain untimed and provide an equivalent keyboard/text response path; and
7. avoid names, grades, disability details, health information, or other private data.

Do not solve this by adding more familiarity questions. Select probes that expose different evidence dimensions. For example, one prompt might ask for a direct interpretation, another for the relation that justifies it, and a third for use after the same information moves from prose to a table. The exact unit content is outside this lesson, so describe the operations without inventing a science answer key.

## Submission frame

Use five short headings:

- Probes
- Evidence record
- Provisional decision rules
- Routes and uncertainty
- Accessibility-equivalent operation

Then stress-test the redesign with two fictional patterns: high-confidence inaccuracy and low-confidence accurate adaptation. Explain why the state and the calibration flag differ in each pattern.

This is transfer because you must diagnose the weakness of an instrument, select the evidence model, and adapt it under privacy and access constraints. Merely renaming Alder, Birch, and Copper would not solve the design problem.
