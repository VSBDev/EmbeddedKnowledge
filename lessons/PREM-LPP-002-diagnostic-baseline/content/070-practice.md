# Fade the classification support

Attempt each stage before reading its feedback. The support decreases while the target operation stays fixed: classify, justify, preserve uncertainty, and route.

## Stage 1: completion problem

:::{check}
:id: practice-completion

A learner produces the relevant relation, succeeds on the familiar form, gives only a memorized slogan as explanation, and has not yet tried a variation. Complete the reasoning:

“The state is provisionally ______ because ______. The missing evidence is ______. The next probe should ______.”
:::

**Feedback.** Provisionally **fragile/unresolved** is the conservative routing label because some usable knowledge appears but the record lacks the explanation and variation needed to justify secure. This does not establish that adaptation would fail; no adaptation probe was attempted. A useful next probe changes the representation while preserving the relation and asks for a reason before feedback. If you chose secure, identify which observation established bounded adaptation; none was supplied.

## Stage 2: supported comparison

:::{check}
:id: practice-supported-comparison

Compare two records.

- **Juniper:** three accurate familiar responses, no explanation or variation, high confidence.
- **Kite:** two accurate independent responses, a defensible explanation, one successful bounded variation, low confidence.

Classify each node, then state the one observation that most strongly distinguishes the classifications.
:::

**Feedback.** Juniper is provisionally **fragile/unresolved** pending an explanation-and-variation probe; repeated familiar accuracy alone does not establish adaptable knowledge, but the missing probe is not a failed probe. Kite supports provisionally secure performance with a calibration flag. The strongest distinction is not confidence but the independent explanation plus bounded adaptation.

## Stage 3: independent mixed case

:::{check}
:id: practice-independent-case

Morrow attempts four prompts. The first is blank. The second is correct after a terminology definition is supplied. The third is correct without a cue and includes a partial explanation. The fourth changes the representation and is incorrect. Confidence rises from low to high across the four prompts. Without using a numeric average:

1. classify the node;
2. cite two observations that support the classification;
3. name one uncertainty; and
4. prescribe a two-step retry route.
:::

**Feedback.** Fragile best preserves the mixed record: relevant knowledge becomes accessible, but access is partly cue-dependent, reasoning is incomplete, and the representation change fails. A good uncertainty asks whether terminology caused the first blank or whether the model itself was unavailable. A proportionate route first contrasts the two representations with a worked explanation, then fades support into two fresh unaided cases separated in time. Rising confidence adds a calibration concern but does not change the state.

## Retry rule

Do not replay the same wording until it feels familiar. Create or request an equivalent case with a new surface form, state the classification before feedback, and compare the new evidence with your previous error. If you repeatedly confuse unfamiliar and fragile, ask first whether **any usable relevant model** appeared. If you confuse fragile and secure, ask whether the model survived **independent explanation and bounded variation**.
