# Baseline mastery evidence

Complete the four items in `assessment.json` without notes on the first attempt. The assessment is untimed and retakeable. Commit each response before viewing its answer logic. A retry uses an equivalent response record or redesign context, not the identical answer sequence.

## Item map

1. `item-classify-pattern` — classify a cue-dependent pattern.
2. `item-separate-confidence` — preserve performance evidence while recording a calibration flag.
3. `item-build-baseline` — classify three nodes, justify uncertainty, and route each one without averaging them into a learner label.
4. `item-transfer-redesign` — replace a familiarity-only survey with an accessible evidence-producing baseline.

The first two items test high-value boundaries. The third collects the complete classify–justify–route performance. The fourth tests whether you can select and adapt the model rather than reproduce the worked example.

## Mastery and retry

Use the analytic rubrics in `assessment.json`. A mastery-ready response must classify from observed evidence, explain why competing states fit less well, preserve a meaningful uncertainty or next probe, and choose a proportionate route. The transfer response must also preserve low stakes, privacy, and an equivalent untimed keyboard/text operation.

The two selected-response items contribute to the 25-point aggregate only. `item-classify-pattern` earns 2 points only for `option-pattern-fragile`; every other choice earns 0. For `item-separate-confidence`, the performance criterion earns 2 points when `option-confidence-secure` is selected and neither distractor is selected, and the calibration criterion earns 1 point when `option-confidence-flag` is selected and `option-confidence-ignore` is not selected. Therefore the exact correct pair earns 3 points; the secure option alone earns 2; the calibration flag alone earns 1; secure plus either distractor earns 0; the calibration flag plus the fragile distractor earns 1; the calibration flag plus the ignore distractor earns 0; and any response containing only distractors earns 0.

For this draft gate, the provisional mastery condition is at least 20 of 25 points overall, at least 6 of 10 points on both `item-build-baseline` and `item-transfer-redesign`, and no criterion receiving zero in either of those two open-response rubrics. The selected-response items affect only the aggregate; a single miss there is not an automatic non-mastery decision. The open-response component minimums and no-zero rule prevent selected-response success from compensating for missing classification reasoning or transfer. These conditions remain candidate authoring decisions until independent review; meeting them is not evidence of delayed retention or real-world effectiveness.

If an item is incorrect, use its common-error feedback to identify the failing distinction:

- no usable model versus some usable model;
- familiar accuracy versus independent bounded adaptation;
- performance evidence versus confidence; or
- classification versus a permanent label.

Return to the matching scene, construct a fresh fictional record that crosses that boundary, and retry with the new record. Do not lower the standard or memorize an option ID.
