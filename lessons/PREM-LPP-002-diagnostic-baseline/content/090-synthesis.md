# Keep the label provisional and the route active

The whole model can be reconstructed as four verbs:

**Probe → observe → classify → route.**

Probe before feedback. Observe access, accuracy, reasoning, adaptation, and calibration separately. Classify one node provisionally from the pattern. Route the learner to initial instruction, targeted strengthening, or delayed verification, then let fresh evidence revise the label.

| State | Minimum defensible interpretation | Route | Do not infer |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Unfamiliar | No usable response model appears yet | Initial explanation and complete model, then an explained attempt | Inability, low potential, or a personal trait |
| Fragile | Some knowledge is accessible but incomplete, cue-dependent, inconsistent, weakly explained, not adaptable, or unresolved because a secure-discriminating probe is missing | Contrast failed and successful forms, or collect the missing probe; then fade support and retry with variation | That nothing is known, that an unattempted operation failed, or that more rereading alone is enough |
| Secure | A pattern of accurate independent reasoning survives bounded variation | Advance provisionally and schedule delayed varied retrieval | Permanent mastery, retention, or universal transfer |

Confidence adds a calibration flag. It never replaces the state evidence.

## Recovery route

If the model itself is unfamiliar, reread the operational definitions in `scene-concept`, cover the table, and reconstruct each state from memory. Then rework Alder, Birch, and Copper with the five evidence dimensions visible.

If your classifications remain fragile, make a two-column comparison: “some usable model” separates unfamiliar from fragile; “independent explanation plus bounded adaptation” separates fragile from secure. Classify two fresh cases without the columns, explain the distinction aloud or in text, and retry the assessment with an equivalent form.

If performance is secure, do not use immediate repetition as the only check. Schedule a fresh classification after a delay and carry the baseline record into `topic-orientation-mastery-and-retrieval`, where it becomes input to a mastery and retrieval plan. The Premed graph deliberately places that outcome downstream of this diagnostic-baseline outcome.

If access to a response format caused the gap, change the medium—such as table to ordered sentences, speech to keyboard, or pointer selection to named options—while preserving the classification operation. A format barrier is not evidence that the knowledge node is unfamiliar.
