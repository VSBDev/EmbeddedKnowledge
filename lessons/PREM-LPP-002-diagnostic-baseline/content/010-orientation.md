# A baseline is a routing tool

Two learners can produce the same total score for different reasons. One may not recognize a node's notation; another may recognize it and reproduce a familiar step but lose the relation when the representation changes. A single total hides the difference, so it cannot choose a proportionate next route by itself.

In this lesson, a **diagnostic baseline** (`term-diagnostic-baseline`) is a low-stakes sample of what is currently accessible. Its job is to answer a bounded routing question: should this one knowledge node receive initial instruction, targeted strengthening, or a brief verification before the learner advances? The baseline is evidence for a decision, not a grade or a description of the learner as a person.

You will learn to:

1. classify a node as unfamiliar, fragile, or provisionally secure from a varied response record;
2. justify the classification while preserving uncertainty and separating confidence from performance; and
3. choose a next route and repair an inaccessible or familiarity-only diagnostic.

This lesson does **not** administer the full Premed entry diagnostic, establish delayed retention, diagnose a learning difference or health condition, award credit, or make an admissions decision. It teaches how to interpret a small fictional or self-generated evidence record without entering private educational or health data.

## Route through the lesson

First retrieve the curriculum-map prerequisite without notes. Then construct the probe → evidence → state → route model, watch a complete worked example, classify new cases before feedback, and complete an instrument-redesign transfer task. Every task is untimed and retakeable with an equivalent case.

## Accessibility and alternatives

All essential relationships appear in source-order prose and tables. No distinction depends on color, position, sound, motion, hover, dragging, handwriting, or speed. You may enter responses by keyboard, speech-to-text, dictation to a scribe, or another structured text route. If a table is awkward in your access mode, read each row as a sentence in column order: node, observed response, explanation, variation, confidence. The reasoning standard remains the same.
