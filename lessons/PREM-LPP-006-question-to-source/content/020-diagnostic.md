# What are you actually trying to find?

This check is ungraded. Use it to choose your route through the lesson.

Consider this fictional need:

> I need to prepare a short explanation of whether retrieval practice helps university biology learners remember material after a delay.

Before reading further, write brief answers to three prompts.

1. What form should the answer take: a first orientation, a synthesis of several studies, one original study, a recommendation, or a set of candidate citations?
2. Which two to four ideas would a relevant item have to represent?
3. Is “PubMed” a document type, a search service, or both?

## Route from your attempt

- If you can state an answer product, name at least two necessary ideas, and distinguish a document from a service that locates documents, continue to the next scene.
- If any prompt is blocked, use the recovery route below. A gap here is routing information, not failure.

## Recovery route

Rewrite the need as two lines:

```text
I need [kind of answer] about [subject or population] and [process, exposure, or intervention]
in order to [use of the answer].
```

Then underline nouns or short noun phrases that determine whether an item would be relevant. Put each different idea in its own pair of brackets. Do not worry about database syntax. Carry the bracketed draft into the next scene and compare it with the decision table there.
