# Carry a search plan forward

Use the five decisions as a compact handoff record:

| Decision | Record | Failure signal | Next action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Knowledge need | What must be known and why | The use is vague or unlimited | Rewrite “I need ___ in order to ___” |
| Answer product | Orientation, synthesis, original report, recommendation, or candidate citations | The selected source cannot produce that form | Change the product or starting source |
| Starting source | Document type or discovery environment plus functional justification | Container and document type are confused | Name each separately |
| Concept blocks | Two to four relevance-determining ideas with candidate variants | An essential idea is missing or a variant changes meaning | Repair one block |
| Revision signal | Translation or sample observation tied to one change | Several changes are made with no diagnosis | Restore the prototype and change one block |

The chain is iterative rather than magical: a source choice can reveal that the answer product was poorly stated, and a result sample can reveal that a concept was missing. The fixed boundary is that retrieval produces candidates, not a quality judgment.

## Mastery evidence

Complete the open assessment only after you can:

- justify a source by the answer product it supplies;
- construct concept blocks without turning every sentence word into a term;
- revise a prototype from an observed signal; and
- state why ranking, inclusion, or full-text availability does not settle appraisal.

Retakes use changed contexts and are untimed. If one objective remains weak, practise only that decision with a new knowledge need before retaking the integrated item.

## Delayed retrieval

Before `PREM-02.02`, retrieve the difference between source function and source authority. Before `PREM-02.03`, reconstruct the five-step chain, then use the later lesson to add documented syntax, controlled vocabulary, and inclusion criteria. This lesson supplies the conceptual plan; it does not claim to supply a comprehensive search.

## Recovery route

Return to the row matching your error, take its single next action, and test the repaired decision in a different context. If your need is high stakes, complex, rare, interdisciplinary, or still unproductive after one reasoned revision, pause and consult an information specialist or responsible domain professional rather than widening the search indefinitely.
