# Transfer the method to a public-health brief

A fictional regional public-health team must prepare a dated briefing on text-message reminders for adolescent vaccination appointments. It needs both a synthesis of intervention evidence and any current recommendation governing official reminder messages in its jurisdiction. Your task is to design the search route, not to recommend care.

Without copying the worked example, produce:

1. two answer-product statements, one for evidence and one for recommendations;
2. a starting document type or discovery route for each product;
3. two to four concept blocks for the evidence question;
4. the jurisdiction and date checks for the recommendation route;
5. one translation or result-sample signal and the single block you would revise;
6. one sentence preserving the retrieval-versus-appraisal boundary.

## Self-check after attempting

A defensible response separates a systematic review from a current jurisdiction-specific guideline rather than forcing both needs into one source. The evidence blocks preserve the population, reminder intervention, appointment context, and attendance outcome; reasonable answers may combine or defer a block if the choice is justified. The recommendation route names issuer, jurisdiction, population, and currency as checks. A valid revision changes one block in response to an observed mismatch. The boundary sentence says that located candidates still require authority, methods, relevance, and quality appraisal.

This is genuine transfer because the task coordinates two answer products and adds jurisdiction and currency constraints absent from the worked example.

:::{callout}
:kind: boundary

Do not infer a vaccination recommendation, communication policy, or expected effect from this fictional task. A real public-health brief requires current authorized guidance, domain expertise, and full evidence appraisal.
:::
