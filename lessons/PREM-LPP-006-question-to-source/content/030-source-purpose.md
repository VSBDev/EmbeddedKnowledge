# Match the answer product to a starting source

A **knowledge need** states what you need to know and why. An **answer product** states what form of information would satisfy that need now. Naming the product first prevents a category error such as expecting a citation database to act like a reviewed recommendation.

## A functional starting map

This table is a starting heuristic, not a ranking of trustworthiness.

| Answer product needed now | Plausible starting source | Function of that source | Boundary to preserve |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Orientation to established terms and relationships | A current textbook, handbook, or reference work | Organizes principles and definitions for consultation | Check date, scope, and later evidence rather than treating an overview as the final word |
| A synthesis of a defined body of research | A systematic review or another explicit evidence synthesis | Uses stated methods to identify and combine evidence relevant to a question | The review's eligibility criteria and date determine what it can cover |
| Original methods, observations, and results | A primary research report, such as a clinical or observational study report | Reports one investigation rather than primarily combining prior studies | One study cannot automatically establish a general conclusion |
| A recommendation for a setting or jurisdiction | A current guideline from the responsible organization | Gives directions or principles for decisions within a declared scope | Confirm population, jurisdiction, issuer, and date before applying it |
| Candidate records from the biomedical literature | A bibliographic database such as PubMed | Searches citation records and publication types using the concepts supplied | Retrieval and ranking do not establish individual article quality |

Bookshelf and PubMed illustrate why the rows must not be collapsed. Bookshelf is an archive that contains several document types, including reference works, systematic reviews, and guidelines. PubMed is a discovery environment that makes literature records searchable. The container or search service is not the same thing as the document type it holds or locates.

## Make the choice explicit

Use three sentences:

1. “I need a **[answer product]** because I must **[use]** the answer.”
2. “I will start with **[source type or discovery environment]** because its function is **[function]**.”
3. “Before using any candidate, I still need to check **[scope, authority, date, methods, or quality]**.”

If you need two products, use two routes. For example, a synthesis of effects and a current jurisdiction-specific recommendation are not interchangeable outputs.
