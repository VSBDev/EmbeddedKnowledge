# Demonstrate mastery and schedule the next retrieval

The mastery check asks you to do more than attach names. You will classify mechanisms, interpret missingness, distinguish whole-study publication from within-study reporting, and audit a new evidence path.

Complete the four items in `assessment.json` without consulting the lesson on your first attempt:

1. `item-map-six-biases`
2. `item-interpret-equal-attrition`
3. `item-distinguish-reporting-publication`
4. `item-transfer-acoustic-archive`

The check is untimed and retakeable. A useful mastery target is at least 13 of 16 points, including at least 4 of 5 points on the transfer item. This target is a routing rule for this lesson, not evidence that one score proves durable mastery.

## Feedback and retry route

After an attempt, use the item-level reasoning and common-error feedback in the assessment file.

- If you miss two or more labels, rebuild the evidence pipeline from memory and redo the independent mixed set.
- If you treat equal attrition percentages as equal bias, compare reasons and early outcome values for missing versus retained cases before retrying.
- If you merge selective outcome reporting with publication bias, write “within one study” and “across whole studies” above two columns and sort the evidence.
- If your transfer response lists labels without mechanisms, rewrite each as “Because ___ determined whether ___ was included or measured, the estimate of ___ could ___.”
- If you guess direction, state what is known about omitted cases. If nothing is known, say the direction is unresolved and request the deciding record.

Retake with the alternate cases embedded in the assessment feedback rather than replaying the same answer sequence.

## One-minute synthesis

Bias detection is a reconstruction problem. First state the target. Then follow the cases through availability, entry, measurement, retention, reporting, and publication. Name a bias only after locating the mechanism. Treat overlapping labels as a map, not a contradiction. State a direction only when the omitted, mismeasured, or hidden results justify it. Finally, choose a check that reaches the same stage as the suspected distortion.

## Boundaries and safe use

This lesson supports critical appraisal of research evidence. It does not diagnose misconduct, prove that a reported effect is false, replace a domain expert's appraisal, or provide a universal statistical correction. The examples contain no personal data and require no clinical decision, physical procedure, or self-experiment.
