# Perpendicular lengths do not simply add

A fictional locator reports an object 12 units east and 5 units north of the origin. Its software reports a straight-line distance of 17 units.

The components are perpendicular, so they form the legs of a right triangle. The distance $d$ is

$$d=\sqrt{12^2+5^2}=\sqrt{169}=13,$$

not $12+5$. The hypotenuse must be longer than either leg but shorter than their sum; 13 passes both bounds.

This lesson uses a four-part geometric audit: label the quantities and angle units, choose a relation that matches the shape, solve, then check lengths, signs, scale, and units.

## Starting check

- If every length doubles, by what factor does area change?
- Is $180^\circ$ equal to $\pi$ radians or $2\pi$ radians?
- In a right triangle, can a leg be longer than the hypotenuse?

If the spatial description is difficult, list the points and perpendicular relationships in text before writing an equation.
