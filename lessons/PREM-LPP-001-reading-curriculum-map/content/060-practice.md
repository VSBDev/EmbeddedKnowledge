# Fade the map-reading support

These fictional fragments isolate the map-reading operation. Attempt each prompt before reading its feedback.

## Completion problem

Complete the hierarchy: domain → ______ → topic. Then state which layer should hold an independently tracked observable outcome.

**Feedback after an attempt:** insert *module*; the topic is the atomic outcome. If you chose domain, you grouped too broadly. Next, practise locating the topic's stable ID rather than relying on its title.

## Supported route filter

Route R is declared to be instrumented and to include the common core. Two topics have `pathway: ["R"]`; three other topics have `core: true` because their requirement is `portable-core`; and one topic has only `pathway: ["S"]` with `core: false`. Which sets enter Route R before prerequisite ordering?

Hint: route relevance and learning readiness are separate decisions.

**Feedback after an attempt:** because R is explicitly instrumented, combine the R-tagged topics with the three `core: true` topics. Do not include the S-only, `core: false` topic. Common-core membership comes from `core`/`requirement: portable-core`, not from a `portable-core` route tag. Without the instrumentation premise, the same union would be only a content filter. Your next step is still to inspect prerequisites; a relevant topic may remain unavailable.

## Independent edge selection

A fictional learner has demonstrated Topic K. Topic L lists K as a prerequisite. Topic M has only a cross-link to K. Select the next topic that the fragment justifies and name the relation that warrants your choice.

**Feedback after an attempt:** L is justified because its stated prerequisite is met. The cross-link makes M worth inspecting but cannot establish readiness. If you selected M, return to the edge-type translation in **The map's grammar**.

## Mixed label discrimination

A topic is marked `evidenceConfidence: medium`, carries a `sourceTags` entry from an exam outline, and is tagged for a pathway. Write one warranted conclusion and two conclusions that the labels do not warrant.

**Feedback after an attempt:** a warranted conclusion concerns the topic's placement in the named route and the recorded evidence family. Do not infer that the scientific content is only “medium true,” that every institution requires it, or that the learner has met its prerequisites.

For an independent scored attempt, complete `item-map-order`, `item-cross-link-order`, and `item-label-boundary` in `assessment.json`. Retry with the equivalent fragments described in the recovery route rather than memorizing option positions.
