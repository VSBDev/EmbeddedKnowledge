# A map is a decision tool

You open a curriculum map because you need to decide what to learn next. The useful question is not “Where should I click?” but “Which identity, scope label, and dependency justify my next step?”

In this lesson you will practise three actions:

1. locate a learning outcome inside a larger structure;
2. separate study-order links from other relationships; and
3. turn a route goal and current prerequisite state into a short plan.

No specialist science knowledge is assumed. You will work with text and tables, so drawing or visually tracing a network is optional.

:::{callout}
:kind: boundary

This lesson teaches how to read the EmbeddedKnowledge curriculum map. It does not determine whether a school will admit you, replace a current institution-specific prerequisite audit, or certify that you have mastered any science outcome.
:::

## Deliberate exclusions

You will not learn the scientific content inside the mapped topics, calculate course completion, or build a personal admissions strategy. Those tasks require later lessons, demonstrated outcome evidence, or current information from the institution concerned.
