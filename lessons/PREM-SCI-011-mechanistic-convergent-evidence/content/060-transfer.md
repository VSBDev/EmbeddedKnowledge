# When the field result disagrees

The worked example moved from a molecular interaction to a greenhouse survival outcome. This transfer changes the causal structure: the intervention changes an **environmental cue**, while the proposed mechanism concerns a **receptor**. You must decide which link a null result actually tests.

All organisms, sites, substances, measurements, and results below are fictional. This is a document-based reasoning task, not an instruction to conduct field or laboratory work.

## The Harbor-7 bundle

Researchers ask whether a dissolved cue, C, triggers shell thickening in a marine snail through receptor Harbor-7.

1. **Molecular line:** purified C binds Harbor-7, but not a Harbor-7 variant with a changed binding pocket.
2. **Organismal line:** under a constant aquarium concentration of C, snails with reduced Harbor-7 expression show little shell thickening, while control snails thicken their shells. Growth rate is similar between groups.
3. **Population line:** shore sites with higher average C have snails with thicker shells. Wave exposure and predator abundance also differ among sites.
4. **Field intervention:** cages at six shore sites are randomly assigned to receive C or vehicle at dawn. Shell thickness after four weeks does not differ detectably. A validation sample shows that sunlight and water exchange reduce C to below the assay's detection limit within one hour on most dosing days.

## Your task

Without looking back at the Tide-1 claim, produce four outputs.

1. **Narrow results:** write one sentence for what each line supports before integration.
2. **Conflict diagnosis:** state whether the field result contradicts Harbor-7 binding, the constant-exposure organismal result, field relevance, or some combination. Explain.
3. **Calibrated claim:** name strength, scope, pivotal evidence, uncertainty, and what the evidence does not establish.
4. **Discriminating study:** propose one ethical, supervised study design in supplied-data form. State the two rival explanations and the different result each predicts. Do not propose collecting live specimens or releasing substances yourself.

Pause here until you have written all four.

## Feedback and one defensible solution

The molecular line supports binding under the assay conditions. The organismal line supports a Harbor-7-dependent response to a maintained concentration of C in the tested aquarium system. The population line supports a field association but cannot separate C from waves and predators. The field intervention supports no detectable effect of the **brief dawn assignment as implemented**; it does not show that the snails experienced the sustained C exposure used in the aquarium.

The null field result therefore weakens a claim that one dawn dose changes shell thickness under ordinary field loss processes. It does not directly refute receptor binding or a response to maintained C. The exposure-validation result explains why the two interventions may have tested different treatment conditions.

A calibrated claim is:

> The evidence provides **moderate support** that Harbor-7 participates in shell thickening when the tested snails receive a maintained aquarium concentration of C. Molecular binding and the Harbor-7 reduction study are pivotal. The shore association is compatible but confounded, and the randomized field assignment produced no detectable effect under rapidly declining exposure. The bundle does not establish that natural variation in C causes field shell differences or that a brief field pulse engages the proposed pathway. A supervised field study that verifies and maintains a prespecified C exposure, while masking outcome measurement, would distinguish “the pathway does not operate in the field” from “the original field intervention did not deliver the relevant exposure.”

For the discriminating study, the rival predictions must separate:

- **delivery failure:** maintained, verified exposure should restore the shell response;
- **field-context failure:** even maintained, verified exposure should not produce the aquarium response.

That design still requires ecological safety, permissions, and specialist supervision. For this lesson, analysing a supplied synthetic dataset is the complete equivalent route.

## Boundary case

Change one fact: suppose C remained at the prespecified concentration throughout the field study, the assay could detect the relevant biological response, and follow-up was complete. The null result would then weigh more strongly against generalizing the aquarium effect to those field conditions. It still would not undo the molecular binding observation; it would narrow the biological consequence of that binding.

If your first claim said “the field trial proves Harbor-7 is irrelevant,” revise the **scope**. If it ignored the field result, revise the **relevance** audit. If your next study simply repeats the original dawn dose, revise the design so the rival explanations make different predictions.
