# Fade the evidence audit

The worked example supplied every decision. These tasks remove support in stages. Attempt all three before reading the feedback section.

## 1. Complete a calibrated claim

A fictional protein, Glow-2, is being studied in a harmless laboratory alga under low light.

- A molecular assay shows Glow-2 binds a light-sensing partner.
- Two independently edited loss-of-function lines grow more slowly than sibling controls under low light but not ordinary light.
- Across lake samples, the Glow-2 variant is associated with algal abundance, but water temperature was measured only once per lake.
- A randomized restoration experiment returns growth of edited algae toward the control range; the same laboratory made both edited lines and performed the restoration.

Complete this claim in one paragraph:

> The bundle provides ___ support that Glow-2 ___. The pivotal evidence is ___. The lake association ___. Confidence is limited by ___. The claim does not establish ___. A next study should ___ because ___.

Your paragraph must distinguish low-light laboratory growth from abundance in natural lakes.

## 2. Fill only the missing audit cells

Do not rewrite the whole table. Supply the two missing entries.

| Line | Reliability or bias | Relevance | Independence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Molecular binding | Clear negative control | Upstream of growth | Different endpoint, same research programme |
| Edited lines | Two lines, sibling controls | Direct for one algal background | Shares editing workflow with restoration |
| Lake association | **A: ___** | Broad setting but indirect for causation | Different setting and investigators |
| Randomized restoration | Assignment supports the comparison | **B: ___** | Shares cells and laboratory workflow with edits |

Then write one sentence explaining why the randomized restoration is not a completely independent fourth vote.

## 3. Select stronger convergence

Two research teams address whether fictional Shell-3 contributes to shell thickening in a marine snail.

**Bundle P** contains four molecular assays. They use different readouts, but all use the same Shell-3 antibody and the same cultured cell line.

**Bundle Q** contains one molecular assay with a sequence control, a gene-reduction experiment in whole snails, and a population comparison across shorelines. The three results point in the same direction. The whole-snail study uses one laboratory strain, and shoreline temperature may confound the population association.

Choose P, Q, or “cannot compare.” Defend the choice by naming:

1. one reason for greater convergence;
2. one remaining shared or line-specific weakness; and
3. the claim you would allow now.

## Feedback after your attempt

### 1. Completion problem

A defensible answer is:

> The bundle provides **moderate support** that Glow-2 contributes to growth of the tested algal background under low light. The pivotal evidence is the loss of growth in two edited lines and its return after restoration, with the binding assay providing a plausible molecular link. The lake association is compatible but not decisive because the temperature measurement may not represent the relevant exposure. Confidence is limited by shared editing and laboratory dependencies and by uncertain field confounding. The claim does not establish that Glow-2 determines abundance across natural lakes. A next study should repeat target-specific restoration in additional backgrounds with time-resolved light and temperature measurements because that would test both generality and the field alternative.

Other strength words can be defensible if the reasons change with them. An answer that says only “four studies prove Glow-2 causes abundance” ignores scope, dependence, and the difference between growth and abundance.

If your paragraph omitted the population boundary, return to **relevance**: ask whether the study measured the same outcome under the same conditions. If it omitted shared laboratory dependence, return to **independence**.

### 2. Missing cells

- **A:** A single temperature measurement can misclassify the relevant temperature exposure and leave residual confounding.
- **B:** Direct for restoration of growth in the edited background, but generality to other backgrounds and natural lakes remains open.

The restoration and edit are valuable together because reversal strengthens the target story. They are not wholly independent because they share biological material, editing decisions, and laboratory procedures. If you treated them as independent, draw a line between every pair of studies that shares samples, reagents, personnel, instruments, or untested assumptions.

### 3. Stronger convergence

**Bundle Q** offers stronger convergence for a bounded Shell-3 claim because its molecular, organismal, and population approaches have more distinct key biases than Bundle P's antibody- and cell-line-dependent assays. Q is still limited by one organismal strain and possible shoreline confounding. A suitable claim is: “The evidence moderately supports Shell-3 as a contributor to shell thickening in the tested laboratory strain; the shoreline pattern is compatible but not causally resolved.”

“Cannot compare” is defensible only if you explain that essential quality information is missing and therefore refuse to infer from method labels alone. Choosing Q because it has “more levels” without discussing quality and bias earns no credit.

## Retry route

Classify your main error before retrying:

- **Contribution error:** you asked one line to answer a question it cannot see. Restate its narrow result.
- **Weighting error:** you treated sample size or randomization as an automatic trump card. Re-audit reliability and relevance.
- **Dependence error:** you counted shared samples, reagents, or assumptions as independent confirmation. Draw the dependence map.
- **Calibration error:** your conclusion omitted strength, scope, uncertainty, or the next discriminating test. Rebuild the five-part claim.

Retry the missed task with Shell-3 replacing Glow-2, or Glow-2 replacing Shell-3. Preserve the reasoning structure but do not reuse the completed sentence.
