# Public copy standard

Status: adopted for the pre-publication pass on 19 July 2026.

## Audience

EmbeddedKnowledge is written for communities, not a single founding learner. Public copy must remain legible to:

- independent and enrolled learners across different ages, countries, and routes;
- educators, subject experts, learning designers, accessibility reviewers, and librarians;
- open-source contributors and the agents working with them;
- schools, universities, foundations, and other institutions evaluating or reusing the corpus;
- readers who want to inspect the project without enrolling in a course.

The founding motivation may remain in the research history, but it must not define the public product contract or cause agents to optimize for one named person.

## Voice rules

1. Address the public as a community or name the relevant role. Avoid assuming one reader, one life story, one route, or one reason for learning.
2. Prefer the project name or a concrete actor over an undefined “we.” Project voice is acceptable for commitments, but not as a substitute for governance.
3. Distinguish the curriculum map, lesson corpus, platform, and credential status. A complete syllabus is not a complete course corpus.
4. Describe current state precisely: empty, proposed in a pull request, under review, adjudicated, or published.
5. Use institutional language for policy and plain language for navigation. Do not use startup placeholders in public copy.
6. Keep boundaries prominent without making them the project’s identity. EmbeddedKnowledge is an open educational corpus; it is not a university, credential, admission decision, medical qualification, or clinical authority.
7. “Learner” may be singular in a rule that applies to each individual record. That grammatical singular is different from designing the whole project for an audience of one.

## Scope of this document

These rules govern public-facing text. The editorial history of individual revisions is kept
in the repository's commit record rather than restated here, so this file stays a standard
rather than a changelog.
