**Category key:** **NORMATIVE** = what institutions require/assume · **DESCRIPTIVE** = what tests/courses actually cover · **SECONDARY** = final pre-university school curricula · **MATERIAL** = content that teaches it.

---

## The headline finding

There is a **clear consensus core** and the disagreements are **structural, not scientific**.

Everyone agrees the foundation is: biology (cell/molecular + introductory sequence), general chemistry, organic chemistry, physics, first-semester biochemistry, and introductory psychology/sociology — plus mathematics and English composition as coursework.

What nobody agrees on is *how the requirement is expressed*: the US does it school-by-school (drifting toward competencies), the UK does it per-institution with Chemistry A-level **not** universal, and Spain legally requires **none** of biology/physics/chemistry together, steering students by exam weightings instead. That's a governance difference, not a knowledge difference — which is excellent news for us, because it means the underlying knowledge target is stable and we're free to design the structure.

**The single most useful artifact found: the AAMC MCAT content outline.** It is a hierarchical, public, complete topic taxonomy (foundational concepts → content categories → reasoning skills) *with explicit disciplinary weightings and stated depth*. Nothing else in the catalog comes close as a ready-made syllabus skeleton.

### United Kingdom

| Source | What it is | URL | Authoritatively defines | Stability |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Medical Schools Council entry requirements tool | Sector-body aggregator, Autumn 2026 entry, data direct from schools | [link](https://www.medschools.ac.uk/for-students/applying-to-medical-school/entry-requirements/) | Per-course subject and grade requirements for every UK medical school | VOLATILE (annual) |

✅ **Chemistry A-level is NOT universally required.** The tool's own filter splits schools into "Chemistry A level required" vs "Chemistry A level not compulsory" — up to ~34 courses don't require it (Manchester, Southampton, Plymouth, Keele; Newcastle accepts Physics/Maths).

### Spain

| Source | What it is | URL | Authoritatively defines | Stability |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| PAU reform (Moncloa, upd. 27 May 2026) | Government statement of admission-grade mechanics | [link](https://www.lamoncloa.gob.es/serviciosdeprensa/notasprensa/educacion-fp-deportes/paginas/2024/pau-prueba-acceso-universidad.aspx) | How the nota de admisión is computed | STABLE (RD 534/2024, in force since June 2025) |

## 2. DESCRIPTIVE — what standardized outlines actually cover

### The MCAT content outline — our best skeleton

| Source | URL | Stability |
|---|---|---|
| "What's on the MCAT Exam?" full outline | [PDF outline](https://students-residents.aamc.org/prepare-mcat-exam/whats-mcat-exam-pdf-outline) · [web](https://students-residents.aamc.org/prepare-mcat-exam/whats-mcat-exam) | STABLE (framework unchanged since the 2015 revision) |
| Bio/Biochem section overview | [link](https://students-residents.aamc.org/whats-mcat-exam/biological-and-biochemical-foundations-living-systems-overview) | STABLE |
| Chem/Phys section overview | [link](https://students-residents.aamc.org/whats-mcat-exam/chemical-and-physical-foundations-biological-systems-section-overview) | STABLE |
| Psych/Soc section overview | [link](https://students-residents.aamc.org/whats-mcat-exam/psychological-social-and-biological-foundations-behavior-section-overview) | STABLE |

✅ It is "a complete description of the competencies you are responsible for knowing", organized as **foundational concepts → content categories → scientific inquiry and reasoning skills**, across exactly four sections: Biological & Biochemical Foundations of Living Systems; Chemical & Physical Foundations of Biological Systems; Psychological, Social & Biological Foundations of Behavior; Critical Analysis and Reasoning Skills (CARS requires no specific content knowledge). Ten foundational concepts ("big ideas") in total.

✅ **Disciplinary weightings — the single most syllabus-useful data in the catalog:**

| Section | Composition |
|---|---|
| **Bio / Biochem** (59 q, 95 min) | intro biology 65% · first-semester biochemistry 25% · gen chem 5% · orgo 5% |
| **Chem / Phys** (59 q, 95 min) | gen chem 30% · biochemistry 25% · intro physics 25% · orgo 15% · intro biology 5% |
| **Psych / Soc** (59 q, 95 min) | intro psychology 65% · intro sociology 30% · intro biology 5% |

> ⚠️ Percentages are AAMC-approximate ("rounded to the nearest 5%, will vary from one test to another"), not fixed.

✅ **Stated depth** — this is the honest answer to "how deep does premed go?": biology, organic and inorganic chemistry, and physics at **introductory college level**; biochemistry at **first-semester** level; psychology and sociology at **first-semester** level.

✅ **Reasoning outweighs recall.** Every science section allocates only ~35% to Knowledge of Scientific Concepts, versus Scientific Reasoning and Problem-solving 45%, Research Design 10%, and Data-based Statistical Reasoning 10%.
> **This is the most important pedagogical finding in the whole run.** The canonical US premed benchmark is *already* two-thirds reasoning. It independently validates the anti-exam design — and it means statistics and research methods are core content, not extras.

⬜ Section-internal weightings for finer mapping: Bio/Biochem = FC1 55% / FC2 20% / FC3 25%; Chem/Phys = FC4 40% / FC5 60%; Psych/Soc = FC6 25% / FC7 35% / FC8 20% / FC9 15% / FC10 5%.

### UCAT (UK)

| Source | URL | Stability |
|---|---|---|
| UCAT test format and scoring | [link](https://www.ucat.ac.uk/about-ucat/test-format-and-scoring/) | STABLE (2025 format) |

✅ **The UCAT is an aptitude test and examines no science content at all.** Four subtests since 2025 (Abstract Reasoning was removed): Verbal Reasoning, Decision Making, Quantitative Reasoning, Situational Judgement. Quantitative Reasoning is ~GCSE-level numeracy; Situational Judgement "does not require medical or procedural knowledge."

## 3. SECONDARY — final pre-university year, by country

### Spain — Bachillerato (LOMLOE)

| Source | URL |
|---|---|
| Real Decreto 243/2022 (consolidated, upd. 2026-07-02) | [BOE](https://www.boe.es/buscar/act.php?id=BOE-A-2022-5521) |
| Biología 2º Bach. evaluation criteria | [educagob](https://educagob.educacionfpydeportes.gob.es/curriculo/curriculo-lomloe/menu-curriculos-basicos/bachillerato/materias/biologia/criterios-eval-segundo-curso.html) |
| EBAU Biología topic breakdown (U. Cantabria) | [PDF](https://web.unican.es/consejo-direccion/vcestudiantes/DocumentosEBAU/Contenido/BIOLOGIA.pdf) |

✅ RD 243/2022 defines the **Ciencias y Tecnología** modality (Art. 8.1) — the Medicina track. In 2º Bachillerato students take Matemáticas II plus **two chosen from six** (Biología, Dibujo Técnico II, Física, Geología y Ciencias Ambientales, Química, Tecnología e Ingeniería II). **No student is legally required to have taken all three sciences.** The de facto Medicina itinerary is Bio + Química (EBAU ponderaciones ~0.2), with Física commonly dropped — a weighting effect, not a legal prerequisite. Anexo II fixes per-subject competencias, criterios de evaluación and *saberes básicos*.

⬜ **2º Bachillerato Biología has exactly six blocks:** A. Las biomoléculas · B. Genética molecular · C. Biología celular · D. Metabolismo · E. Biotecnología · F. Inmunología. Notably **no** ecology, human anatomy/physiology, or evolution block.

⬜ **Spain goes deeper than the Anglo systems in three specific places** — the most interesting secondary-school finding in the run:
- **Metabolism at university depth**: glycolysis, fermentation, β-oxidation, Krebs cycle, electron transport chain, oxidative phosphorylation, with comparative energy-yield calculations.
- **Modern genetic engineering by name**: PCR, restriction enzymes, molecular cloning, **CRISPR-Cas9** — beyond most legacy premed prerequisite lists.
- **A full mandatory immunology block**: innate vs. specific, humoral vs. cellular, natural/artificial and passive/active immunity, phases of infectious disease, immune pathologies — content that in the US/UK is typically first-year university or medical school material.

> *Enseñanzas mínimas are a state floor (60% of hours; 50% in co-official-language regions); autonomous communities complete the rest.*

### United Kingdom — A-levels

| Board | Source | URL |
|---|---|---|
| AQA | A-level Biology 7401/7402 specification | [PDF](https://filestore.aqa.org.uk/resources/biology/specifications/AQA-7401-7402-SP-2015.PDF) |
| OCR | A Level Biology A (H420) specification v4.1 | [PDF](https://www.ocr.org.uk/Images/687834-download-a-level-specification.pdf) |
| Pearson Edexcel | Biology A (Salters-Nuffield) 9BN0 | [PDF](https://qualifications.pearson.com/content/dam/pdf/A%20Level/biology-a/2015/specification-and-sample-assessment-materials/9781446930885-gce2015-a-bioa-spec.pdf) |

⬜ **AQA Biology — eight topics:** (1) Biological molecules (2) Cells (3) Organisms exchange substances with their environment (4) Genetic information, variation and relationships between organisms (5) Energy transfers in and between organisms (6) Organisms respond to changes in their internal and external environments (7) Genetics, populations, evolution and ecosystems (8) The control of gene expression. AS covers 1–4; full A-level adds 5–8.

⬜ **OCR Biology A — six modules:** (1) Development of practical skills (2) Foundations in biology (3) Exchange and transport (4) Biodiversity, evolution and disease (5) Communication, homeostasis and energy (6) Genetics, evolution and ecosystems.

⬜ **Edexcel Biology A — eight context-led topics:** Lifestyle, Health and Risk · Genes and Health · Voice of the Genome · Biodiversity and Natural Resources · On the Wild Side · Immunity, Infection and Forensics · Run for your Life · Grey Matter.

⬜ **Two constants across all three boards, both worth stealing:**
- **A mandatory practical endorsement** — minimum 12 assessed practical activities (AQA lists them explicitly: enzyme kinetics, mitotic index from stained root-tip squashes, water potential by dilution series, membrane permeability, dissection, aseptic technique, chromatography of leaf pigments, chloroplast dehydrogenase activity…), teacher-assessed against cross-board Common Practical Assessment Criteria, reported separately from the grade.
- **A quantitative floor** — at least 10% of marks must require mathematics at higher-tier GCSE level or above, applied in biological context.

⬜ No formal prior qualification is required for entry to A-level Biology; GCSE science is the assumed floor.

### International Baccalaureate

⬜ Source: [IB DP Sciences (Group 4)](https://ibo.org/programmes/diploma-programme/curriculum/sciences/). Every DP student must take at least one Group 4 science, chosen from six (biology, chemistry, physics, computer science, design technology, sports/exercise/health science). **Environmental Systems and Societies can satisfy Group 4**, so an IB diploma holder could in principle reach medicine having taken none of biology, chemistry or physics. All Group 4 sciences include a compulsory collaborative interdisciplinary project on the environmental, social and ethical implications of science.
> ⚠️ **Gap:** the authoritative HL topic lists live in the per-subject briefs, not this hub page. Not yet fetched.

### United States — Advanced Placement

⬜ Sources: [AP Central courses](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses) · [AP Biology CED](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/media/pdf/ap-biology-course-and-exam-description.pdf).

**AP Biology — eight units:** (1) Chemistry of Life (2) Cells (3) Cellular Energetics (4) Cell Communication and Cell Cycle (5) Heredity (6) Gene Expression and Regulation (7) Natural Selection (8) Ecology. Built around four "big ideas" that spiral across all units: Evolution, Energetics, Information Storage and Transmission, Systems Interactions. College Board equates it to a **full-year college introductory biology course for majors**.

Two design features worth stealing: **six mandatory science practices** (concept explanation, visual representations, questions and methods, representing and describing data, statistical tests and data analysis, argumentation — including chi-square testing and confidence intervals), and a mandate that **25% of instructional time be hands-on inquiry lab work**.

**AP Physics is four separate courses** (Physics 1 and 2 algebra-based; Physics C Mechanics and E&M calculus-based) — US pre-university physics exists at two distinct mathematical tiers. AP maths relevant to premed: Precalculus, Calculus AB, Calculus BC, Statistics.

### Germany — Abitur

⬜ Source: [KMK Bildungsstandards für die Allgemeine Hochschulreife](https://www.kmk.org/themen/qualitaetssicherung-in-schulen/bildungsstandards/bildungsstandards-und-allgemeine-hochschulreife.html). National standards exist for **Biologie, Chemie and Physik (adopted 2020)**, plus Deutsch, Mathematik and continued foreign language (2012) — six subjects total. Enforced through a **shared national pool of Abitur exam tasks** across all 16 Länder, so they describe content actually tested.
> ⚠️ The overview page doesn't state the binding exam year; widely reported as the 2025 cycle for sciences, but must be confirmed from the individual resolution PDFs.

### France — Baccalauréat

⬜ Source: [arrêté MENE1921249A](https://www.education.gouv.fr/bo/19/Special8/MENE1921249A.htm) (signed 19 July 2019, effective from the 2020 school year).

The **terminale physique-chimie specialty is 6 hours/week and explicitly designed to prepare students for higher education in experimental sciences and medicine** — the clearest statement of medicine-directed intent found in any secondary curriculum. Four themes: constitution and transformations of matter; movement and interactions; energy; waves and signals.

Chemistry includes Brønsted acid-base, pH, titration by pH-metry and conductimetry, kinetics, radioactive decay, spontaneous evolution criteria, multi-step organic synthesis. Physics includes Newton's second law with equations of motion, Kepler's laws, introductory fluid dynamics, first law of thermodynamics, and wave/quantum optics (diffraction, interference, photon, photoelectric effect).

### ⚠️ Two licensing/availability facts that affect product decisions

1. **OpenStax licensing must be verified title by title.** A NonCommercial or ShareAlike term can be incompatible with the adopted CC BY 4.0 lesson corpus or constrain downstream reuse. Keep such works as references rather than adaptation substrates unless rights review confirms compatibility; prefer CC BY, public-domain, or separately permissioned source material for derivatives. This is a publication gate, not a later business-model decision.
2. **Khan Academy's MCAT collection was announced as retained "until 2026."** Its availability right now is uncertain — the live URL currently serves a bot-challenge page. Since it's the single largest free MCAT-aligned corpus, **this is a dependency worth verifying and archiving early.**

## 5. Agreement and disagreement

### The consensus core (this is the syllabus spine)

Every normative and descriptive source converges on:

| Domain | Depth |
|---|---|
| Biology (cell/molecular + intro sequence) | Introductory college |
| General chemistry | Introductory college |
| Organic chemistry | Introductory college |
| Physics | Introductory college, algebra-based sufficient |
| Biochemistry | First-semester college |
| Psychology + sociology | First-semester college |
| Mathematics (incl. statistics) | Varies; statistics is universally implied |
| Scientific reasoning, research design, data analysis | **~65% of MCAT assessment** |

### The contested edges (these are our design decisions)

1. **Course-based vs. competency-based.** US schools are drifting from fixed course lists to competencies; the AAMC says "some" without quantifying. Spain's 2024 PAU reform pushes ≥70% open-ended questions. Both trends point our way — but *how far* is unresolved.
2. **Which science is mandatory.** UK: Chemistry not universal (but *a* science is). Spain: legally none of the three, steered by exam weightings. US: chemistry most-required by frequency. **Conclusion: the union of bio + chem + physics is what medicine actually assumes; no single system requires all three.**
3. **Where biochemistry lives.** Required by many US schools, 25% of two MCAT sections — but absent from most secondary curricula as a named subject (except Spain, which teaches metabolism at near-university depth). It sits in the gap between school and university, which is exactly where a premed course should live.
4. **Immunology, genetic engineering, anatomy/physiology.** Spain mandates immunology and CRISPR at secondary level; the Anglo systems don't. A&P is absent from the MCAT outline *as a standalone section* but is the natural bridge to actual medicine. **Correction (adjudicated later):** organ-system material is real and substantial in the outline under content category 3B, assigned to introductory biology; only *anatomy* specifically is weakly represented. The syllabus and graph follow the adjudicated position, not this line.
5. **Labs and practical work.** UK A-levels require ≥12 assessed practicals; AP requires 25% lab time; many US schools require lab components. **The MCAT outline contains none of this** — and it's the hardest thing for an online platform to replicate. Open design question.
6. **Calculus and statistics.** Required by some US schools, outside the MCAT outline, variable in secondary curricula. Statistics is clearly load-bearing (10% of every MCAT section); calculus much less so.

### Hidden assumptions worth naming

The reasoning-heavy MCAT weighting, the lab endorsements, and AP's six science practices all point at something no curriculum lists as content: **scientific method, experimental design, data and statistical literacy, and argumentation**. Roughly two-thirds of the canonical benchmark tests these rather than recall. A syllabus built only from topic lists would miss the majority of what's actually assessed.
